To our guests traveling from the US, don't forget your passports!

Toronto's main airport is Pearson Airport (code: YYZ) is located about 35-45+ minutes NW of Toronto. To get from the airport to Toronto, there is the option to rent a car, rideshare (Uber/Lyft), taxi, or take the very convenient train called the UP Express. Check out this site for transportation alternatives.
There's a scenic route for those who prefer to drive across the US/Canada border. You can fly to Buffalo Niagara International Airport (code: BUF) and drive about 2.5 hours N to Toronto. The domestic flights to Buffalo are typically more cost-effective than flying internationally to Toronto. However, be sure to factor in additional travel time for the border crossing. For transportation alternatives to renting a car, check out this site or Buffalo Airport Shuttle which offers transfers from the airport to Toronto.

Things to Do

Home of the 2019 NBA Champions-the Raptors, Maple Leafs, Blue Jays, CN Tower, rich history, and international cuisine, there's no shortage of things to do in Toronto! Americans, just remember it's pronounced "tuh-ronno" not "toe-ron-toe".

CN Tower

Highlighting the city's skyline, the iconic CN Tower will be hard to miss. You can tour the very top and even go outside on their Edge Walk and lean over the edge. Check out more info and reserve your tickets ahead of time on their website.

The Distillery District

Known for a lot more than just our wedding spot, the Distillery District has boutique shops, restaurants, bars, and specialty cafes you can spend a day exploring.

Shopping at St Lawrence Market

Timeout.com says "This downtown market has been going strong since 1803, and today, it is home to over 120 vendors selling everything from fresh-from-the-farm veggies to mouth-watering meals to gourmet bread straight out of the oven."

Visit Toronto Island

Niagara Falls

If you're willing to take the 2 hour drive, Niagara Falls is south of Toronto. A walk around the falls at sunset or at night can be gorgeous. You can also take a ferry through them, though prepare to get wet!

Around Niagara Falls is a thriving industry of haunted houses, a Butterfly Conservatory, Botanical Gardens, and even Niagara Falls Casino.

Eat

Great meals can be found all over the greater Toronto area (GTA) at any price range. Some guides are Timeout.com Toronto, Michelin star restaurants, but Americans, you need to checkout Tim Horton's at least once. Don't worry, you'll probably see a few while you're here.

Drink

Foodism.com's guide can be a starting point. Americans, all booze are sold in separate liquor stores called "LCBOs" so you can't just run to a 7-Eleven late night. If you just want beer, there are also aptly named "Beer Stores" that can take care of you.
